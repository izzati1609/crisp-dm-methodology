Forecasting Project: Retail Sales

Scenario: Inventory Optimization at FreshMart Supermarket

Problem Statement

FreshMart experiences frequent stockouts of milk and bread during weekends while overstocking on weekdays. The current manual ordering process relies heavily on estimation, leading to revenue loss and high food waste.

Phase 1: Business Understanding

This phase focuses on translating business challenges into data science objectives. The key priorities identified are:

  • Primary Objective: Develop a 7-day predictive model to forecast demand for daily essentials.
  • Success Metric: Target an 18-20% reduction in "Out of Stock" situations during peak weekend hours.
  • Outcome: Provide a data-driven ordering strategy to improve inventory efficiency.

Phase 2: Data Understanding

The analysis began by collecting 2 years of historical sales records from the store's Point of Sales (POS) system.

Initial Discovery: Exploring the data revealed that promotions significantly drive sales volume, whereas public holidays often lead to a decrease in local demand as customers travel out of town.

Phase 3: Data Preparation

Before modeling, the raw data underwent several cleaning and transformation steps to ensure quality:

Data Cleaning

Handled missing values and removed outliers from store closure dates.

Feature Engineering

Created "Day of the Week" features to help the model recognize weekend patterns.

Data Selection

Focused on the most recent 12-month period to reflect current shopping trends.

Phase 4: Modeling

The algorithm selected for this project is Multiple Linear Regression.

Technical Rationale: Linear Regression is an effective model for understanding the relationship between independent factors (Promotions, Holidays, Day of Week) and the dependent factor (Units Sold). It provides a clear and interpretable way to forecast sales based on historical trends.

Phase 5: Evaluation

The model was tested by comparing forecasted demand against the actual sales recorded during a test week.

Model Accuracy: 88%

The visualization shows that the model captured the overall trend effectively, particularly the weekend peaks. An 88% accuracy rate is considered successful for initial inventory planning.

Phase 6: Deployment

The final stage involves integrating the model into the store's operations to provide real-world value:

Automation

The system automatically generates a suggested order report every Sunday evening for the Store Manager.

Business Impact

Initial results showed an 18% reduction in stockouts, leading to higher revenue and better customer satisfaction.
